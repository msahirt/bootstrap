Living Room Contemporary Interior Design

Texture play creates interest in this living room.

Simple layout with clean lines: Contemporary interior design comprises no-fuss layouts to create clean fresh settings. High ceilings and tall windows in crisp lines lend the space a sense of lightness.

It necessitates the use of open layouts to create the illusion of a larger space. This means creating more free-flowing multifunctional spaces that are free from excessive decor items yet classy.

The trademark of contemporary style interior design is the use of a neutral colour palette. Shades of white, ivory, beige, cream and taupe take a front seat. These are highlighted by grey and black, and sometimes tan and brown.

Do not mistake this interior design theme for boring. It involves the use of interesting geometric patterns to create unique designs. Think stripes, checks, zig-zag lines, and more on walls, upholstery, carpets, furniture, and even tiles!

For a true contemporary bedroom interior design, stick to one colour and use a mix of its light and dark shades. This creates depth in design. The wooden laminates, curtains, bed covers, and even the rug are colour-coordinated to create a simple vibe. Add a plant with interesting leaves in a basic planter to complete the look.

As shared above, contemporary design utilises natural materials and natural light to make a room look spacious. This bedroom design has both in abundance. The wooden floor and veneer accent wall make the bedroom look cosy and create an interesting contrast against the neutral colours.

The tall windows covered by beige and white curtains bring in ample sunlight to make the bedroom look cheerful and classy. The grey metal table and floor lamp along with the hanging pendant lights in the corner serve as interesting additions.
